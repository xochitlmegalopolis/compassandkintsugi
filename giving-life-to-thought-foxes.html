Stories, poems and literature act as lights and beacons, showing us possibilities for how to breathe life into our own lives. They paint for us roads and exemplars; some of these may be desirable, and others may be better to avoid. Whether true or false or somewhere in between, stories and myths function (among other things) as thought experiments. Not all of what they paint for us is actually or literally feasible; if we want to know what is and what isn’t, we will have to test everything for ourselves and in our own lives. It is also true that what is or is not feasible differs widely according to the person and the circumstances that each individual faces. What is not feasible for us may not be impossible universally. What is feasible and desirable for us may not be feasible or desirable for everyone. Stories allow us to explore landscapes of possibility freely without expensive consequences. However, they vary in accuracy, level of “literal” correspondence and in finesse of detail. They cannot, therefore, be taken as a substitute for real, personal experience. The human mind has such incredible creative power, and the great thing about it is that it is a power that everyone has the potential to develop. But unfortunately, this power is not always used wisely or to good ends. It is not enough to develop it alone: it must be developed in conjunction with a calibrated motivational compass, and a finely-tuned heart. If we think about this power in ourselves — in fact, these three powers working together, also called Attention, Motivation and Emotion — if we think about just how incredible and beautiful that potential is, when used to non-destructive, but to artful ends — why would we let something so precious go to waste? And why would we let it be wasted by others? When I started thinking about that idea, I was very young. I resolved that I would dedicate my life to pursuing the development of that creative art within myself, and that I would let no one else, for any reason, bring harm to its flourishing within me. It wasn’t until after I made this resolution that I would realize how difficult it was to fulfill, how much of the world we live in is structured to thwart it, and how many people — some obviously malicious and others probably genuinely well-intentioned — would try to stand in my way.
