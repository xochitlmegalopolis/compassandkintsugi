So many dreams, like feathered wings caught in the jaws of predators or idol butcher-gods of monstrous visage fizzle and choke, strangled before the clouds they part can breathe their well-loved worlds into existence and as they fade to dust, the future before us blinks out like a far light flickering desperately like a failing heart so that five minutes forward is but dark. Like auroras, once, or forging nebulas their stardust roads arose from quiet calm of slow care and glowed with colors rarely seen. But several centuries and hard choices forced their coursing and gleaming into faded and forgotten voids of mind's night. Here and there a handful of debris cast by a flying comet falls to earth again, glittering with the magic of the icy spark that has outlived oblivion; still able to cause light to dance it revives in the trembling hands. All ghosts of treasures can be reconstructed, after all from traces of evidence wed with seeds of possibility. Despite what some of our well-paid professional destroyers would probably say... But the dead themselves cannot be returned from the grave; so spoke the ancient general Sun Tzu; every act that has laid stardust's heartwrought roads to waste on history's long strand, may the actors rue.
